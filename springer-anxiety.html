Why Separation Anxiety Statistics Matter for Springer Spaniel Owners

Springer Spaniels are among the most people-oriented breeds — a trait bred in over centuries of close fieldwork alongside handlers. Their strong attachment drive makes them exceptional working and companion dogs, but it also predisposes them to separation-related distress. Understanding the prevalence data helps owners recognize early warning signs and intervene before mild anxiety becomes a clinical problem.

Peak Age of Onset and the Critical Intervention Window

Behavioural data shows that separation anxiety in Springer Spaniels most commonly emerges between 6 and 18 months — especially in dogs from working lines. The highest-risk window is the first 12 weeks after adoption or during adolescence when the dog’s attachment bonds intensify. According to the ASPCA separation anxiety guide, early intervention in this window produces significantly better long-term outcomes than treating established anxiety. Activities and ideas grounded in the data

Springer spaniel separation anxiety statistics activities are most effective when they address root causes. Based on current research, the best springer spaniel separation anxiety statistics ideas include:
